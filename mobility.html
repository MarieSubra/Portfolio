International Mobility

During our engineering degree programme, we are required to spend a semester abroad immersed in the country of our choice. We have the option of doing an internship or taking courses at a university and earning credits.

Taiwan

National Taiwan University (NTU) in Taiwan is my first wish for my exchange program. The university offers many courses in its Computer Science & Information Engineering (CSIE) department, particularly in software engineering, a field that interests me greatly. The specialities Advanced Software Technology, Development of App Program for Mobile Device and Software Engineering. Taiwan would also be a great opportunity to improve my Chinese and English. NTU is renowned for its international outlook and the diversity of its students, which will allow me to discover new cultures and progress in an enriching environment.

Hong Kong

As Taiwan, Hong Kong is a particularly dynamic cultural and international center, which will allow me to improve my English and Chinese skills. The city is home to many multinational companies and start-ups, particularly in the engineering sector and computer sciences. I am currently in contact with an engineer working at BNP Paribas, who could help me identify internship opportunities as part of my mobility program.

Australia

The University of Sydney offers several courses open to international students in the fields of engineering and computer science. The specialty that interests me most is software engineering because it covers topics that interest me, such as language compilers, software analyst and engineer, and web developer. I am also interested in the advanced computing specialty because it will allow me to improve my programming and design skills.
